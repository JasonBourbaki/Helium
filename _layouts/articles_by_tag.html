Helium Two: Reality

 

        Helium-2 is an extremely unstable isotope of helium that consists of solely two protons. Its dichotomous structure reminds me of our increasingly polarized society where people shut themselves up from the "others". Researchers have concluded that helium-2 would have been more stable if the strong force was two percent greater. Similarly, humanity would hold together better with 2% more love and understanding.

 

      This isotope of me comments on real-world events and tells stories about lovely people I encountered, in hope to plant a bit more happiness and faith in the readers' hearts.

Helium Three: Future

 

        Helium-3, though stable, makes up less than 1 / 7000 of helium on earth. The moon and the gaseous planets are where it resides. This scarce material provides ideal fuel for nuclear fusion. We need to expand into the outer space and make breakthroughs in fusion to make use of He-3. Therefore, it symbolizes the future, the advancement of science and technology.

 

      This isotope of me explores and contributes to fields that would play a prominent role in the impending era, namely machine learning, virtual reality, and aerospace engineering.
